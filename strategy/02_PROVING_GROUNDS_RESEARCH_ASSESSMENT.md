# Assessment of the supplied Proving-Grounds research

## Overall
Helpful, but it describes a different product: an independent public crash-test lab for AI agents. Do not copy its Next.js/Neon/Vercel leaderboard architecture into the 72-hour PR verifier.

## Directly transferable
- Cryptographically sealed evidence manifests and independent verify CLI.
- Merkle/content hashes, signatures, key rotation and transparency-log thinking.
- Pass^k/consistency rather than single-run confidence.
- Anti-gaming: separate checker, fixture integrity, canaries, held-out variants.
- Public methodology, appeal/correction notes and no-pay-for-result independence policy.
- Badge/CI/MCP distribution loops.
- Failure-to-fixture flywheel and community bounties.
- Procurement-grade evidence dossiers, with careful non-certification disclaimers.
- Spectacle: short replay clips/head-to-head evidence pages.

## Useful later, not Day 1–3
Hosted transparency service, vendor certification, procurement packs, public leaderboard, MCP server, bounty payouts, clip pipeline.

## Not transferable
Phantom Graph research; public agent arena; LLM-as-judge as primary verdict; commerce prompt-injection scenarios; Neon/Blob/Redis stack; browser agent adapters. These solve another product.

## Corrections to supplied research
Several market numbers and “verified” labels should be rechecked before public use. LLM judge reproducibility cannot be guaranteed merely by temperature 0. Cryptographic sealing proves integrity, not correctness. EU AI Act mapping must be presented as evidence support, not legal compliance.
